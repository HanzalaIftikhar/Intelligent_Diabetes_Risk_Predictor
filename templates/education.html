📚 Diabetes Education Center

🩸What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body either doesn't produce enough insulin or can't effectively use the insulin it produces, resulting in elevated blood glucose levels. Over time, high glucose can damage organs and blood vessels.

⚠️Key Risk Factors

  • High glucose levels and family history (genetics)
  • Being overweight or obese (high BMI)
  • Physical inactivity
  • Age above 45 years
  • High blood pressure
  • History of gestational diabetes during pregnancy

🥗Prevention Strategies

  • Maintain a healthy weight through balanced diet
  • Exercise regularly — at least 30 minutes a day
  • Reduce intake of sugary and processed foods
  • Monitor blood glucose levels periodically
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption
  • Get regular health checkups, especially after age 40

💪Healthy Lifestyle Guidelines

  • Eat more fiber-rich foods — vegetables, whole grains, legumes
  • Stay hydrated and limit sugary drinks
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques
  • Get 7-8 hours of quality sleep each night
  • Consult your doctor for regular screening if at risk

⚠️Important Disclaimer

This information is for educational purposes only and does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment.
